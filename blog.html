Google Ads & Keywords

Learning how the keywords and google ads work was really interesting. It never occured to me how difficult it would be to come up with keywords that would actually put your website infront of potential customers. Something that I didn't do at first was put keywords that involved user intent. Most of my first keywords looked like "ux ui", "graphic design", "website design", etc. I was curious to see how my keywords were so I put them in gemini to see what how it thought I was doing with them. One of the main things that it suggested was to add more keywords with user intent, and also change the match type to be more specific. I defaulted everything to broad because that is what was on the example that was provided. For the high intent words I changed the match type to exact match, and for almost all of the others I did phrase match. This all was the purpose of making the google ads campaign run better and be the most effective. I realized that having the idea of what you want your customers to do can really impact the keywords that you choose

Making better Ads

Ads are an important part of getting your business infront of potential customers. Having a good ad campaign is vital to having an effective ad campaign. If your ad isn't effective you will end up spending more money than you will get from your ad. Google has some tools to help you make sure that your ads will work, but another way to see how your ad works is simple trial and error. Using both of these tactics will bring you the best ad for what you need. Being able to use Google's tools to get started, then watching which ad combinations work the best and bring the conversion that you are wanting. As well as providing tools to see how Google says the ad will do, Google also privides ways to track which ad combinations work best. I'm looking forward to continuing to tweak my ads to get the best possible results from the campaign

Quality & Relevance Score

I felt like quality and relevance score really came down to how well thoughout your website is, and how well thoughout everything that links to it is. The discussion example had some pretty obvious and glaring errors in it. The ad was for rigby homes, and it opened to an IF homes page. Some of my classmates mentioned that the link threw them off, but it didn't bother me too much because if you are looking into the Rigby area you have probably seen Idaho Falls and recogize that it is close by. But whoever put the ad together could very easily have done a quick link check to make sure that it opened to the right page. Increasing these scores is really just a game of details. When you make sure the details are right, and are delivering what the user wants, your score will go up. This does mean that you will often need to be checking these details, if you change part of your ad, you need to make sure that doesn't effect the flow of the user.

ROI & Ad Optimization

Learning about tracking your ROI was insightful. Since launching my ad campaign I have been tracking the traffic on my website both on google ads, and through the form submisson process that comes with my hosting site. As I have been monitoring the ad I have noticed that the conversion tracking has been a little off. Google ads shows that the conversion tracking for the form has been triggered 16 times, but so far I have recieved email requests 3 times. After doing a little bit of research I found out that I need to track down and find all the places that the script to track conversions is running, as it will typically run when the page is loaded, not when the form is submitted. I have enjoyed seeing how the ad has been working, and continuing to work on my website to improve the experience of the user so they will be more likely to submit the form to request a design. Using the different tools has helped me to figure out how to optimize my website to also optimize the ads so that I can have the best ROI possible.
