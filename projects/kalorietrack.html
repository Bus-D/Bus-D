Introduction

KalorieTrack is an app design based on a theroretical nutrition app that was owned by Underarmour. It was orginally mainly a fitness tracking app, but after seperating from Underarmour they wanted to rebrand to mainly be a nutrition app.

My Role

For this project I was the designer. I worked with a team of researchers to create personas, userflows, and quick tests to make sure the app functioned as intented.

Discovery Phase

Personas

We worked with three personas for the project.

Hannah
Maria
Howard

Hannah

Hannah values a balanced lifestyle. Because of her 9-5 deskjob she finds it difficult to stay active during the week. Her main goal is to maintain her weight, and knows that keeping track of her nutrition will play a major role in that.

Maria

Maria is a busy working parent, who is trying to lose the extra weight she has gained the last few years. She needs a simple and effective way to track her nutrition. Because of her hectic schedule she doesn't have the time or energy for a complicated tacking app.

Howard

Howard has a love/hate relationship with his current fitness tracking app. He wants to maintain a healthy lifestyle but he claims that work "often gets in the way". He wants an app that works like a tool, and not a coach.

Takeaways
  • There are multiple reasons to use a nutrition tracking app
  • Don't leave out the fitness functionality
  • Make it simple

User Flows

Creating the user flow diagram was really insightful and helpful for the redesign. It helped me begin to think through all that would need to happen for the app to be successful, and what screen I would need to make sure I implemented to ensure a smooth pleasent experience for the user.

Takeaways
  • Can't forget about the transtion screens
  • The home page needs to be able to access every other page with at most 1-2 taps
  • Tab bar is vital to a simple navigation
